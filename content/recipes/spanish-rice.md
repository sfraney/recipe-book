## Ingredients

* 2 cups freshly cooked white rice (still warm works best)
* 4 slices bacon, diced
* 1 small onion, finely chopped
* 4–6 cloves garlic, minced
* 1 cup tomato sauce
* ½ cup frozen peas (optional)
* ½ tsp salt (adjust to taste)
* ½ tsp black pepper
* ½ tsp cumin (optional but good)
* ½ tsp oregano (optional)
* 1–2 tbsp water if needed

## Process

1. **Cook the bacon**
   In a large skillet over medium heat, cook the diced bacon until crisp. Leave the rendered fat in the pan.

2. **Sauté aromatics**
   Add the chopped onion to the bacon fat. Cook 4–5 minutes until soft.
   Stir in the garlic and cook about 30 seconds until fragrant.

3. **Add tomato sauce**
   Pour in the tomato sauce. Stir to combine with the bacon and onions.
   Add salt, pepper, cumin, and oregano if using. Simmer 2–3 minutes to slightly thicken.

4. **Add rice**
   Add the cooked rice and break up any clumps. Stir thoroughly so the tomato sauce coats the rice.

5. **Add peas**
   Stir in the frozen peas. Cook 3–5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until everything is heated through.
   If the mixture seems dry, add a tablespoon or two of water.

6. **Taste and adjust**
   Adjust salt and pepper. Serve hot.

## Notes

* Freshly cooked rice fries better than refrigerated rice for this dish because it absorbs the tomato sauce while staying soft.
* If you want more bacon flavor, reserve some crispy bacon and crumble it over the top when serving.
* A squeeze of lime or a little chopped cilantro works well as a finishing touch.
