## Ingredients

### Tofu
- 1 block (14 oz) firm or extra-firm tofu
- Salt
- 1 tbsp cornstarch
- 2–3 tbsp neutral oil

### Sauce
- 1½ tbsp soy sauce
- 1–2 tsp oyster sauce
- ¾ tsp sugar (or substitute)
- ¾ tsp rice vinegar
- ~3 tbsp water or light stock
- ¾ tsp cornstarch

### Stir Fry
- 10–12 oz mushrooms, sliced
- 1 head bok choy, cut into bite-sized pieces
- 2 tsp ginger, julienned
- 2 cloves garlic, sliced
- 1 tbsp neutral oil
- Small drizzle sesame oil (to finish)

---

## Method

### 1. Prepare Tofu
Cut tofu into ¾–1 inch cubes. Lay on towels and press lightly for 10–15 minutes. Season lightly with salt and toss with cornstarch.

### 2. Fry Tofu
Heat oil over medium-high heat. Add tofu in a single layer and cook undisturbed for 3–4 minutes until golden. Turn and repeat until most sides are browned. Remove and set aside.

### 3. Cook Mushrooms (Dry Phase)
Heat pan over high heat with no oil. Add mushrooms and cook until they release and evaporate their moisture.

### 4. Brown Mushrooms
Add oil and continue cooking until mushrooms are well browned and concentrated.

### 5. Add Aromatics
Add ginger and garlic. Stir-fry for 10–15 seconds until fragrant.

### 6. Add Greens
Add bok choy and stir-fry 1–2 minutes until just wilted.

### 7. Combine and Finish
Return tofu to the pan. Stir sauce and add it. Toss for 1–2 minutes until thickened and glossy. Remove from heat and finish with a small drizzle of sesame oil.

---

## Notes

- Do not marinate tofu; moisture prevents browning.
- Mushrooms must cook dry first to avoid steaming.
- Keep heat high throughout to maintain separation of phases.
- Serve with grain mix on the side rather than underneath.
