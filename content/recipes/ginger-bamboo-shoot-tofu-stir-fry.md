## Overview

A clean, ginger-forward stir fry built around bamboo shoots and celery for crunch. Light sauce, aromatic, and balanced without relying on cabbage.

---

## Ingredients (Serves 3)

- 10 oz firm tofu, pressed and cubed
- 1 tbsp fresh ginger, finely minced (use more if you want it dominant)
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1/2 medium onion, sliced
- 1 cup canned bamboo shoots, drained and rinsed
- 1/2 cup celery, sliced on a bias
- 1 small carrot, thinly sliced
- 1.5 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tsp Chinkiang vinegar
- 1 tbsp canola (or rapeseed) oil
- 1 tsp sesame oil
- 1/4 cup water (plus more as needed)

**Optional base:**
- Shirataki noodles (preferred)
- or small portion black rice

---

## Prep

1. Press tofu and cut into ~1-inch cubes.
2. Mince ginger and garlic.
3. Slice vegetables.
4. Rinse bamboo shoots to remove any canned taste.

---

## Instructions

1. Heat canola oil in a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat.
2. Add tofu and cook until browned on multiple sides, ~6–8 minutes. Remove and set aside.
3. Add onion and cook ~2 minutes until starting to soften.
4. Add ginger and garlic; stir-fry ~30–45 seconds until fragrant.
5. Add bamboo shoots, celery, and carrot. Stir-fry 3–4 minutes.
6. Return tofu to the pan.
7. Add soy sauce, Chinkiang vinegar, and 1/4 cup water. Toss to combine.
8. Cook another 2–3 minutes until lightly sauced and heated through.
9. Finish with sesame oil and toss.

---

## Notes

- Don’t skimp on ginger; it defines the dish.
- Keep the sauce light—this should not be saucy or heavy.
- If the pan dries out, add small splashes of water instead of more oil.
- For more browning, let tofu sit undisturbed briefly between turns.

---

## Serving

Serve hot over shirataki noodles or a small portion of black rice.
