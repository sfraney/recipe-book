## Ingredients

- 6 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs (about 2½–3 lb total)
- Kosher salt
- Freshly ground black pepper
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 12–15 asparagus spears (about ¾ lb), trimmed

#### Porcini Pan Sauce
- 19 g dried porcini mushrooms
- ¾ cup boiling water
- ½–¾ cup low-sodium chicken stock
- 1 small clove garlic, minced
- ½ teaspoon fresh thyme (or small pinch dried)
- 1 tablespoon unsalted butter
- 1 tablespoon chopped fresh parsley (optional)

---

## Process

### 1. Rehydrate the Porcini

Place dried porcini in a heatproof bowl.  
Pour ¾ cup boiling water over them and soak 15–20 minutes.

Lift mushrooms out of the liquid (do not pour it out).  
Strain soaking liquid through a coffee filter or fine cloth to remove grit.  
Finely chop the rehydrated mushrooms. Set aside.

---

### 2. Prepare the Chicken

Preheat oven to 425°F.

Pat chicken thighs completely dry.  
Season generously on both sides with salt and pepper.

Heat an oven-safe skillet over medium heat. Add olive oil.

Place thighs skin-side down and cook 8–10 minutes without moving, until skin is deeply golden and most fat has rendered.

Flip thighs skin-side up and transfer skillet to oven.  
Roast 15–20 minutes, until internal temperature reaches about 175°F in the thickest part.

Remove chicken to a plate to rest.  
Carefully pour off excess fat from the skillet, leaving about 1 tablespoon.

---

### 3. Roast the Asparagus

While the chicken finishes in the oven:

Toss asparagus lightly with olive oil, salt, and pepper.  
Spread on a sheet pan.

Roast at 425°F for 8–12 minutes, until just tender and lightly browned.

---

### 4. Make the Porcini Pan Sauce

Return skillet to medium heat.

Add chopped porcini and sauté 1–2 minutes.

Add garlic and thyme. Cook about 30 seconds until fragrant.

Add:
- ¼–⅓ cup strained porcini soaking liquid  
- ½ cup chicken stock  

Scrape up browned bits from the pan.

Simmer 3–5 minutes until slightly reduced.  
Adjust thickness by adding a bit more stock or reducing further.

Remove from heat and stir in butter until glossy.  
Adjust salt and pepper to taste.

---

## Serve

Plate two thighs per person with 4–5 spears of asparagus.  
Spoon porcini pan sauce over the chicken.  
Garnish with parsley if desired.
