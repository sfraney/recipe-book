## Overview

A savory, umami-forward tofu stir fry using fermented black beans and a controlled amount of dried shiitake. Potassium is managed by limiting shiitake and keeping tofu moderate, while cabbage provides bulk.

---

## Ingredients (Serves 3)

- 10 oz firm tofu, pressed and cubed
- 3–4 dried shiitake mushrooms
- 2 cups cabbage, sliced
- 1/2 cup celery, sliced
- 1/2 medium onion, sliced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tbsp fermented black beans, lightly rinsed and chopped
- 1.5 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tsp Chinkiang vinegar
- 1 tbsp canola oil (or rapeseed oil)
- 1 tsp sesame oil
- 1/4 cup water (plus more as needed)

**Optional base:**
- Shirataki noodles (preferred for low potassium)
- or small portion black rice

---

## Prep

1. Rehydrate shiitake mushrooms in hot water for ~20 minutes. Slice thinly. Reserve soaking liquid.
2. Press tofu and cut into ~1-inch cubes.
3. Chop vegetables and aromatics.

---

## Instructions

1. Heat canola oil in a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat.
2. Add tofu and cook until browned on multiple sides, ~6–8 minutes. Remove and set aside.
3. Add onion and cook ~2 minutes until starting to soften.
4. Add garlic and fermented black beans; stir-fry ~30 seconds until fragrant.
5. Add shiitake, cabbage, and celery. Stir-fry 3–5 minutes.
6. Return tofu to the pan.
7. Add soy sauce, Chinkiang vinegar, and 1/4 cup water (or reserved mushroom liquid). Toss to combine.
8. Cook another 2–3 minutes until everything is heated through and lightly sauced.
9. Finish with sesame oil and toss.

---

## Notes

- Keep shiitake quantity modest to control potassium.
- If the pan dries out, add small splashes of water rather than more oil.
- Mushroom soaking liquid adds depth but can be skipped if you want tighter potassium control.
- For stronger flavor, increase black beans slightly—but they are potent, so adjust carefully.

---

## Serving

Serve hot over shirataki noodles or a small portion of black rice.
