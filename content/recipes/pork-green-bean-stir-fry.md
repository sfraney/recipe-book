## Ingredients

### Main
- 1 lb green beans, trimmed and thoroughly dried
- ½–¾ lb ground pork
- 4–6 oz mushrooms, sliced
- 1–2 cups cabbage, thinly sliced

### Aromatics
- 3 cloves garlic, chopped
- 1 tbsp ginger, minced

### Sauce
- 1½ tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tsp sugar (or substitute)
- 1 tsp rice vinegar
- 2–3 tbsp water
- 1 tsp cornstarch (optional, for light thickening)

### Oil
- 2–3 tbsp neutral oil (adjust based on pork fat rendered)

---

## Method

### 1. Cook Pork
Heat wok over high heat. Add pork and cook, breaking it up, until browned with some crisp edges.

Remove pork from pan.  
- Leave ~1–2 tbsp rendered fat  
- Discard excess liquid if present  

---

### 2. Dry-Fry Green Beans
Return pan to high heat.

Add green beans and cook, turning occasionally, until:
- skins blister and wrinkle  
- beans are tender but still slightly firm  

This takes ~6–10 minutes.

Remove and set aside.

---

### 3. Cook Mushrooms
Add a small amount of oil if needed.

Add mushrooms and cook until:
- moisture releases and cooks off  
- mushrooms begin to brown  

---

### 4. Aromatics
Add garlic and ginger. Stir-fry 10–15 seconds until fragrant.

---

### 5. Add Cabbage
Add sliced cabbage and stir-fry briefly (1–2 minutes) until just softened but not wilted.

---

### 6. Combine
Return green beans and pork to the pan.

Add sauce (stir first if using cornstarch).  
Stir-fry over high heat until:
- liquid reduces  
- everything is lightly coated  

---

### 7. Finish
Taste and adjust seasoning. Serve immediately.

---

## Notes

- Keep the pan hot and avoid crowding to maintain proper blistering of the beans.
- Mushrooms should be cooked until their moisture evaporates to prevent steaming the dish.
- Cabbage should remain slightly crisp; overcooking will release too much water.
- This dish is lightly sauced; it should not be wet.
