### Ingredients

- 1 1/2 lb bok choy (about 3 medium heads)
- 2 cloves garlic, finely minced
- 1 tbsp fresh ginger, finely minced
- 1–2 tsp fermented black beans, lightly rinsed and mashed
- 1 tbsp neutral oil (avocado or other high-heat oil)
- 1–2 tbsp water
- Salt, to taste
- Optional: 1 tsp rice vinegar

### Preparation

1. **Prep the bok choy**  
   Trim the base and separate stems from leaves.  
   Slice stems into 1/4-inch pieces. Leave leaves in large bite-sized pieces.

2. **Prepare aromatics**  
   Mince garlic and ginger finely.  
   Rinse fermented black beans briefly to remove excess surface salt, then mash into a coarse paste.

### Cooking

1. Heat a wide skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add oil.
2. Add bok choy stems with a small pinch of salt. Stir-fry 2–3 minutes until slightly tender but still crisp.
3. Add garlic, ginger, and mashed black beans. Stir constantly for 30 seconds until fragrant.
4. Add bok choy leaves and 1–2 tbsp water. Cover and steam 60–90 seconds until leaves wilt.
5. Remove lid and cook uncovered briefly to evaporate excess moisture.

### Finish

Taste and adjust salt if needed.  
Add a small splash of rice vinegar if you want a sharper finish.  

Serve immediately while bright and crisp.
